Pathophysiology

The Story

During embryologic development, the infundibular septum — the wall that divides the outflow of the right and left ventricles — deviates anteriorly and to the right. That one anatomic mistake cascades into four features, which is where the name comes from.

Because the septum is displaced, the pulmonary outflow narrows (you get pulmonary stenosis), the aorta gets pulled over the wall so it sits straddling both ventricles (overriding aorta), blood has to find another way out and punches through the septum (VSD), and the right ventricle, pumping against the stenosis, muscles up (RV hypertrophy).

The functional result: when the pulmonary stenosis tightens — during crying, feeding, exertion, or dehydration — blood takes the path of least resistance and shunts right to left through the VSD, skipping the lungs entirely. Deoxygenated blood goes out the aorta. The baby turns blue. This is a tet spell.

Squatting increases systemic vascular resistance, which pushes blood back toward the lungs and relieves the spell. Toddlers with unrepaired TOF learn this instinctively — they'll squat during play. Boards love this detail.

Clinical Presentation

How They Show Up

👶
Age at presentation
Usually 2–6 months as PS progresses. Severe forms present in neonates as ductal-dependent cyanosis.
💙
Cyanosis
Intermittent, worsens with crying, feeding, exertion. "Blue baby" during stress.
🫃
Squatting
Toddlers squat reflexively during play to relieve spells. Increases SVR → ↓ R-to-L shunt.
🎵
Murmur
Harsh systolic ejection at ULSB (from PS, not VSD). Intensity ↓ during tet spell.
🫁
Clubbing
Develops in older unrepaired children from chronic hypoxemia.
📉
Growth
Poor weight gain, fatigue with feeding, delayed milestones if severe.
Diagnosis

Workup Ladder

Step 1 · Suspect
Pulse oximetry + 4-extremity BPs
Universal newborn CCHD screen catches many. Any cyanotic infant → sat on pre/post-ductal limbs.
Step 2 · Screen
Chest X-ray
"Boot-shaped heart" (coeur en sabot) from RV hypertrophy lifting the apex. Decreased pulmonary vascular markings. 25% have right-sided aortic arch.
Step 3 · Screen
ECG
Right axis deviation and RV hypertrophy. Can be normal in mild disease.
Step 4 · Confirm
Echocardiogram
Gold standard. Shows all four defects, quantifies PS severity, identifies associated lesions (right aortic arch, coronary anomalies).
Step 5 · Pre-op
Cardiac MRI / catheterization
Before surgical repair to map coronary anatomy and pulmonary arteries.
Management

Acute vs Definitive

🚨
Acute Tet Spell
  • 1stKnee-to-chest position — ↑ SVR, reduces R→L shunt
  • 2nd100% oxygen — pulmonary vasodilator
  • 3rdMorphine — calms infundibular spasm, reduces crying
  • 4thIV fluids — increases preload
  • 5thPhenylephrine — ↑ SVR if refractory
  • 6thBeta-blocker (propranolol/esmolol) — relaxes infundibulum
🏥
Definitive Care
  • NeonatePGE1 if ductal-dependent (keeps PDA open for pulmonary flow)
  • BridgeBT shunt (modified Blalock-Taussig) if too small for full repair
  • 4–6 moComplete surgical repair — VSD patch + RVOT enlargement
  • HomeParents taught knee-to-chest for spells
  • Long-termLifelong cardiology f/u; pulmonary valve replacement often needed in adulthood
Board Pearls

✨ Don't Miss These

Classic stem
"Toddler squats during play" → TOF until proven otherwise.
Don't confuse
During a tet spell, the murmur gets quieter (less flow through PS). Counterintuitive but tested.
Genetic association
15% have 22q11.2 deletion (DiGeorge). Check for hypocalcemia, facial features, thymic aplasia.
Pink vs Blue TOF
"Pink tet" = mild PS, predominantly L→R shunt. Presents later, may look like acyanotic CHD.
Endocarditis prophylaxis
Indicated 6 months post-repair or if residual defects. Not routinely after that.
CXR finding
Boot-shaped heart + decreased pulmonary vascular markings. Not increased — that's left-to-right shunts like VSD.
