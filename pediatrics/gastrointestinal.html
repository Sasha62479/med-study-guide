Surgical vs Medical
First question for any GI complaint: does this child need an OR, or can we treat medically? Red flags (bilious vomiting, peritoneal signs, bloody stools) push you right.
High Yield
🔪
Surgical / Obstructive
Bilious vomiting · distension · peritoneal signs · don't delay imaging
1.
Pyloric Stenosis
3–6 wk first-born male · projectile non-bilious vomit · olive + hypochloremic alkalosis
 2.
Malrotation / Volvulus
Neonate + bilious vomiting = surgical emergency. UGI series. "Corkscrew"
 3.
Intussusception
6 mo–2 yr · colicky pain + currant jelly stool · air/contrast enema reduces
 4.
Appendicitis
Periumbilical → RLQ · anorexia · kids <5 yr often perforate before dx
 5.
Hirschsprung Disease
Delayed meconium >48 h · aganglionic segment · rectal biopsy = gold std
 6.
Meckel Diverticulum
Rule of 2s · painless lower GI bleed <2 yr · Meckel scan (99m-Tc)
💊
Medical / Inflammatory
Diarrhea · dehydration · stool studies · hydration first
1.
Acute Gastroenteritis
Rotavirus, norovirus · ORS first-line · ondansetron for vomiting
 2.
GERD
"Happy spitter" vs pathologic · upright post-feed, thickened feeds, PPI if severe
 3.
IBD (Crohn / UC)
Weight loss, bloody stool, growth failure · fecal calprotectin · scope
 4.
Celiac Disease
Chronic diarrhea + FTT + bloating · tTG-IgA · biopsy confirms
 5.
C. difficile Colitis
Post-antibiotic diarrhea · don't test <1 yr (colonization) · PO vanc

✨ Board Pearls
Don't Miss These
Bilious vomiting
In a neonate, bilious vomiting is malrotation with volvulus until proven otherwise. Immediate UGI series. Time matters — bowel ischemia happens fast.
Pyloric stenosis labs
Classic hypochloremic, hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis from losing HCl. Rehydrate and correct electrolytes before OR. Pyloromyotomy is elective, not emergent.
Currant jelly stool
Classic sign of intussusception — but it's a late finding. Don't wait for it. A colicky toddler with lethargy between episodes is enough to image.
Meckel's Rule of 2s
2% population · 2 feet from ileocecal valve · 2 inches long · 2 years typical age · 2 tissue types (gastric, pancreatic). Painless lower GI bleed in a toddler.
Delayed meconium
Failure to pass meconium >48 hours after birth → think Hirschsprung. Rectal biopsy showing absent ganglion cells confirms.
Celiac screening
tTG-IgA + total IgA is first-line. If total IgA low, use tTG-IgG or DGP-IgG. Child must be eating gluten when tested. Biopsy confirms.
