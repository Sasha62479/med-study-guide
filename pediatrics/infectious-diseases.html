Fever Workup by Age
Age defines the workup — the younger the infant, the more aggressive the evaluation. Febrile neonates are a medical emergency.
High Yield
0–28 days
Neonate
▸
Full sepsis workup — CBC, blood cx, UA + urine cx, LP
▸
Admit + empiric abx — ampicillin + gentamicin (± acyclovir if HSV concern)
▸
Cover GBS, E. coli, Listeria
▸
Fever = T ≥ 38.0°C rectal
29–60 days
Young Infant
▸
Apply risk stratification (Rochester / Philadelphia / PECARN)
▸
CBC, blood cx, UA, procalcitonin; LP if high-risk or ill-appearing
▸
Low-risk + well-appearing → may observe off abx
▸
Bugs shift toward E. coli, Strep pneumo
3–36 mo
Toddler
▸
Focused H&P — look for a source
▸
UA if female <24 mo, male <12 mo uncirc, or male <6 mo circ
▸
Well-appearing + vaccinated + no source = likely viral
▸
Watch for occult pneumonia if high fever, high WBC
≥3 yr
Child
▸
Workup driven entirely by history & exam
▸
Most viral — symptomatic care, reassurance
▸

Viral vs Bacterial
The first bedside question: does this need antibiotics? Pattern recognition for the common ones.
Core Content
🦠
Viral Infections
Supportive care · exanthems · gradual onset · often seasonal
1.
Roseola (HHV-6)
High fever 3–5 d → fever breaks → pink rash appears. Infants 6–24 mo
 2.
Measles
Cough, coryza, conjunctivitis + Koplik spots → cephalocaudal rash
 3.
Varicella (Chickenpox)
Crops of lesions in different stages · "dew on a rose petal"
 4.
Hand, Foot & Mouth
Coxsackie A16 · oral ulcers + palm/sole vesicles · supportive
 5.
Fifth Disease (Parvovirus B19)
"Slapped cheek" → lacy body rash. Aplastic crisis in sickle cell
 6.
Mononucleosis (EBV)
Fatigue, splenomegaly, exudative pharyngitis · avoid contact sports
💊
Bacterial Infections
Antibiotics change outcome · abrupt onset · localized source
1.
Strep Pharyngitis (GAS)
Centor/McIsaac · rapid strep + throat cx · amoxicillin 10 d
 2.
Scarlet Fever
GAS + sandpaper rash + strawberry tongue · Pastia's lines
 3.
Impetigo
S. aureus / GAS · honey-crusted lesions · mupirocin topical
 4.
Cellulitis
Warm, red, tender, spreading · cover MRSA if purulent
 5.
UTI
E. coli >> others · cath or clean catch · consider VUR if recurrent
 6.
Bacterial Meningitis
Fever + headache + neck stiffness · empiric abx before LP if delayed

✨ Board Pearls
Don't Miss These
Febrile neonate
Any infant ≤28 days with T ≥ 38.0°C rectal gets a full sepsis workup + admission + empiric antibiotics. No exceptions. No "looks well so I'll send them home."
Kawasaki timing
IVIG within 10 days of fever onset drops coronary aneurysm risk from ~25% to ~4%. Fever ≥5 days + 4 of CREAM = treat, don't wait.
Fever + rash that appears when fever breaks
Classic for roseola (HHV-6). Toddler had 3 days of high fever, then fever gone and now rash → reassure the parents.
Avoid aspirin
In viral illness (esp. influenza, varicella), aspirin risks Reye syndrome. Exception: Kawasaki disease, where aspirin is part of the treatment.
Spleen + mono
EBV mononucleosis → avoid contact sports × 3–4 weeks to prevent splenic rupture. Ampicillin → diffuse rash (pathognomonic but not allergic).
Parvovirus + sickle cell
Parvo B19 infects erythroid precursors → aplastic crisis in SCD patients. Admit for transfusion support.
