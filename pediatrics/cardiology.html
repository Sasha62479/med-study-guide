Congenital Heart Disease
Classified by whether they shunt right-to-left (cyanotic) or left-to-right (acyanotic).
High Yield
💙
Cyanotic CHD
The 5 T's — Tetralogy, Transposition, Truncus, TAPVR, Tricuspid atresia
1.
Tetralogy of Fallot
"Tet spells" · boot-shaped heart · squatting relieves cyanosis
 2.
Transposition of Great Arteries
Cyanosis at birth · "egg on a string" · needs PGE1 + balloon septostomy
 3.
Truncus Arteriosus
Single outflow vessel · assoc. DiGeorge (22q11)
 4.
TAPVR
Pulm veins drain to RA · "snowman" sign · obstructed form = emergency
 5.
Tricuspid Atresia
Small RV · L-axis deviation on ECG · needs ASD + VSD to survive
❤️
Acyanotic CHD
L→R shunts · can become cyanotic if Eisenmenger develops
1.
VSD
Most common CHD · holosystolic murmur at LLSB · small ones often close
 2.
ASD
Fixed split S2 · often asymptomatic until adulthood
 3.
PDA
Machine-like continuous murmur · close w/ indomethacin, keep open w/ PGE1
 4.
Coarctation of Aorta
BP ↑ arms / ↓ legs · rib notching · assoc. Turner syndrome

✨ Board Pearls
Don't Miss These
Ductal-dependent lesion
Cyanotic newborn not improving on oxygen? Start PGE1 immediately. Don't wait for echo.
Tet spell
Knee-to-chest position increases SVR, reduces R→L shunt. Parents are taught to do this at home.
BP 4 extremities
Always measure in suspected CHD. Arm >> leg = coarctation.
Fixed split S2
Pathognomonic for ASD. If the split doesn't move with respiration, think ASD.
Kawasaki coronary risk
Treat with IVIG within 10 days to prevent coronary artery aneurysms. Echo at baseline, 2 wks, 6–8 wks.
Innocent vs pathologic
Innocent murmurs are systolic, soft (≤2/6), change with position. Diastolic = always pathologic.
