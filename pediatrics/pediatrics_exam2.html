⚡ Quick Reference

🚨
NEVER examine the oropharynx with a tongue depressor in suspected epiglottitis — can trigger complete airway obstruction. Allow tripod position. Blow-by O2 only.
⭐
Radiating murmur = PATHOLOGICAL. Innocent murmurs (Still's = musical systolic; Venous hum = continuous) do not radiate and vary with position.
💉
Tinea capitis = ONLY tinea requiring ORAL treatment. Griseofulvin or terbinafine × 4–6 wks. Topicals alone are insufficient.
📌
Chronic paronychia: Loss of cuticle + nail dystrophy = pathognomonic (Candida). Scabies: Pruritus persisting weeks after permethrin treatment = expected — NOT treatment failure.

⚙ Five Key Clinical Workups
MyocarditisCXR + ECG + MRI + CBC + enzymes + cultures
Pediatric HTNUA + CMP + Lipid panel
EndocarditisBlood cultures ×3 + echocardiogram
PericarditisEcho + CXR + fluid culture (pericardiocentesis)
KawasakiESR elevated; fever ≥5d + 4/5 criteria
Fever ≤28dFull sepsis workup including LP — always
💊 First-Line Antibiotics
AOMAmoxicillin 90 mg/kg/day ÷ BID
Impetigo (mild)Mupirocin ointment TID × 7–10d
CellulitisCephalexin or amoxicillin
Lyme ≥8yrDoxycycline × 14–21d
RMSF all agesDoxycycline (even <8yr; short course)
GAS pharyngitisAmoxicillin × 10 days
Bacterial sinusitisAmoxicillin; amox-clav if severe
Tinea capitisOral griseofulvin OR terbinafine × 4–6wks

👁 Conjunctivitis DDx (Mahoney 2023)
BacterialMucopurulent; matted AM; H. influenzae #1 (≤70%)
ViralWatery; preauricular LAD; adenovirus #1
AllergicBilateral ITCH; seasonal; pale boggy turbinates
Neonatal CT5d–2wks post-birth; watery → purulent
Neonatal GC2–5d post-birth; severe purulent; lid edema
Contact lensALWAYS treat + refer slit-lamp
🫁 Croup vs. Epiglottitis
Croup soundsBarking cough + hoarseness + stridor
EpiglottitisDrooling + Dysphagia + Distress (3 D's)
Croup CXRSteeple sign
Epiglottitis CXRThumbprint sign
Croup txDexamethasone + racemic epi if severe
Epiglottitis txOR airway — NEVER examine throat
💉 Pneumonia by Age
<1 monthGBS, E. coli, Listeria → Amp + Gent IV
1–3 monthsChlamydia (afebrile, staccato) → Azithromycin
3mo–5yrViral + S. pneumo → Amoxicillin if bacterial
5–18yrS. pneumo + Mycoplasma → Amox ± azithromycin
🧠 High-Yield Pearls & Mnemonics
3 D's
Epiglottitis
Drooling · Dysphagia · Distress
NEVER examine throat!
5 pts
Myocarditis Dx
Resp distress · Poor perfusion · Hypotension · ECG abnormalities · Abnormal CXR
KD Rx
Kawasaki Disease
IVIG 2g/kg + high-dose ASA → low-dose ASA × 6–8wks · Refer cardiology
FBA
Foreign Body Aspiration
Coughing + Wheezing + Unilateral diminished breath sounds
Doxy
Tick-Borne Rx
RMSF + Lyme → doxycycline. RMSF: ALL ages, treat NOW. Short course = no dental staining.
⚡ Traps
Don't Miss!
Amox + sore throat = EBV rash (not allergy!) · Scabies itch ≠ tx failure · Tinea capitis needs ORAL tx
