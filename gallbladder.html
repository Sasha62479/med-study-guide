Gross Anatomy

The gallbladder is a pear-shaped sac (~7-10 cm long, 30-50 mL capacity) on the inferior surface of the right hepatic lobe.

Parts

  • Fundus — blind-ended tip; palpable in cholecystitis
  • Body — main portion
  • Neck — joins cystic duct; has spiral valve of Heister
  • Hartmann's pouch — outpouching at neck; stones can lodge here

Blood Supply

  • Cystic artery — branch of right hepatic artery; within Calot's triangle
  • Calot's triangle — bordered by liver, common hepatic duct, cystic duct; key surgical landmark
🩺 Clinical

Murphy's sign: inspiratory arrest with RUQ palpation — classic for acute cholecystitis.

Microscopic Anatomy

Wall Layers

  • Mucosa — simple columnar epithelium; no muscularis mucosae or submucosa
  • Muscularis — smooth muscle; contracts with CCK
  • Serosa — covers free surface; absent on hepatic surface
Boards Pearl

Rokitansky-Aschoff sinuses — invaginations of mucosa through muscularis. Prominent in chronic cholecystitis.

🩺 Clinical

Charcot's triad (RUQ pain + fever + jaundice) = cholangitis. Reynolds' pentad adds altered mental status + septic shock.

Bile

Composition

  • Bile salts — emulsify fats; conjugated with glycine/taurine
  • Phospholipids (lecithin) — solubilize cholesterol
  • Cholesterol — must stay soluble in bile salt/lecithin micelles
  • Conjugated bilirubin — yellow-green color

The GB concentrates bile 5-10x by absorbing water/electrolytes. CCK (from duodenal I-cells) triggers GB contraction and sphincter of Oddi relaxation.

Boards Pearl

Cholesterol stones (80%): radiolucent; risk = 5 F's: Female, Fat, Forty, Fertile, Fair. Pigment stones: black (hemolysis, cirrhosis) vs brown (infection).

Mnemonic

5 F's for gallstones: Female, Fat, Forty, Fertile, Fair (Northern European). Also: OCPs, rapid weight loss, Crohn disease.

Embryology

GB and extrahepatic bile ducts develop from the hepatic diverticulum (caudal portion / pars cystica) at ~4 weeks.

  • Pars cystica → gallbladder + cystic duct
  • Failure of recanalization → biliary atresia
🩺 Clinical

Biliary atresia: progressive jaundice after 2 weeks, pale stools, ↑conjugated bilirubin. Kasai procedure before 60 days of life is essential.
