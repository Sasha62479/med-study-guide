Anatomy & Physiology

Gallbladder & Bile

A hollow, pear-shaped organ that stores and concentrates bile. Supplied by the cystic artery and closely related to the extrahepatic biliary tree.

1. Gross Anatomy

Structure and Location

  • Hollow, pear-shaped (piriform) intraperitoneal organ
  • Lies on the cystic plate beneath liver segments IVB and V
  • Length: 7-10 cm; Width: 2.5 cm; Volume: 30-35 mL (up to 300 mL if cystic duct obstructed)

Parts

  • Fundus - rounded distal end
  • Body - main portion
  • Infundibulum - narrows from body to neck
  • Neck - connects to cystic duct then common hepatic duct then common bile duct
  • Hartmann's pouch - dilation on ventral aspect proximal to neck; most common site of gallstone impaction

Function

  • Storage of bile (drained from liver via small bile ducts)
  • Concentration of bile: absorbs electrolytes and water
  • Cholecystokinin (CCK) stimulates gallbladder contraction, releasing bile into the 2nd part of the duodenum

Vasculature and Innervation

  • Arterial supply: cystic artery (branch of right hepatic artery)
  • Venous drainage: neck via cystic veins to portal vein; body/fundus via hepatic sinusoids
  • Sympathetic: celiac plexus
  • Parasympathetic: hepatic branch of vagus nerve
  • Sensory: phrenic nerve fibers - referred pain to right shoulder (C3)
Clinical: Cholecystitis refers pain to the right shoulder because the phrenic nerve originates from C3-C5, which also provides somatic sensation to the shoulder.

2. Microscopic Anatomy

  • Wall: mucosa, muscularis, perimuscular connective tissue, serosa (on free surface)
  • Mucosa: simple columnar epithelium with microvilli (aids bile concentration)
  • Rokitansky-Aschoff sinuses: outpouchings of mucosa through the muscularis - seen in cholecystitis and adenomyomatosis
  • No submucosa or muscularis mucosa (distinguishes it from gut)

3. Biliary Tract

Intrahepatic Bile Ducts

  • Bile canaliculi to intrahepatic bile ductules (canals of Hering) to segmental bile ducts to sectional ducts
  • Right posterior duct (VI and VII) + Right anterior duct (V and VIII) = Right hepatic duct
  • Ducts from segments II-IV = Left hepatic duct
  • Right + Left hepatic ducts = Common hepatic duct (CHD)

Extrahepatic Bile Ducts

  • Common bile duct (CBD): CHD + cystic duct, travels in hepatoduodenal ligament, joins pancreatic duct, drains into ampulla of Vater at major duodenal papilla

4. Bile

Produced by the liver, stored in the gallbladder. Aids in digestion, fat absorption, acid neutralization, and excretion of bilirubin and cholesterol.

Composition

  • Bile pigments: Bilirubin (yellow, conjugated by liver), Biliverdin (green, from Hgb breakdown)
  • Primary bile acids: Cholic acid, Chenodeoxycholic acid (from cholesterol; rate-limiting enzyme: cholesterol 7-alpha hydroxylase)
  • Secondary bile acids: Deoxycholic acid, Lithocholic acid (formed by intestinal bacteria)
  • Bile acids conjugated with glycine or taurine = bile salts (water-soluble)
  • Other: phospholipids, cholesterol, water, ions

Functions

  • Emulsification and absorption of fats (including fat-soluble vitamins A, D, E, K)
  • Excretion of bilirubin and cholesterol
  • Antimicrobial effect and IgA transport

Enterohepatic Circulation

  • ~95% of bile acids reabsorbed (distal ileum) via portal circulation back to liver
  • Bilirubin: ~80% excreted as stercobilin in feces; ~20% enters enterohepatic circulation; ~10% excreted by kidney

5. Embryology

  • Derived from the endoderm of the foregut
  • Hepatic diverticulum gives rise to liver, gallbladder, and biliary ducts
  • Cystic duct forms from the caudal part of the hepatic diverticulum

6. Clinical Significance

Biliary Tract Disorders

  • Primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC), Primary biliary cholangitis (PBC)
  • Ascending cholangitis, Choledocholithiasis
  • Cholecystitis (acute and chronic), Biliary colic, Cholelithiasis
  • Porcelain gallbladder, Cholangiocarcinoma
  • Mirizzi syndrome, Biliary atresia, Choledochal cyst, Caroli disease

Bile Disorders

  • Neonatal jaundice, Gilbert syndrome, Crigler-Najjar syndrome
  • Dubin-Johnson syndrome, Rotor syndrome
  • Intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy, Primary bile acid malabsorption
