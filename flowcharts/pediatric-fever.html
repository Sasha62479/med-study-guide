Key Clinical Notes

Antipyretics

  • Acetaminophen: 15 mg/kg q4–6h (max 75 mg/kg/day)
  • Ibuprofen: 10 mg/kg q6–8h (age ≥ 6 months, max 40 mg/kg/day)
  • Do NOT give aspirin to children (Reye syndrome risk)
  • Goal: comfort, not normalization of temp

Red Flag Signs (Any Age)

  • Petechiae or purpura (consider meningococcemia)
  • Stiff neck, bulging fontanelle, photophobia
  • Inconsolable crying, lethargy, poor perfusion
  • Fever > 5 days: consider Kawasaki disease
  • Immunocompromised: lower threshold for workup

Common Sources to Rule Out

  • UTI: most common serious bacterial infection in young children
  • AOM: common in 6 mo–2 yr
  • Pneumonia: tachypnea, retractions, hypoxia
  • Strep pharyngitis: age ≥ 3 yr, use CENTOR/McIsaac criteria

Return Precautions to Give Parents

  • Return if fever > 5 days
  • Return if new rash, especially petechiae
  • Return if child is inconsolable or increasingly lethargic
  • Return if not drinking or signs of dehydration
