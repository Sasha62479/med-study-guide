📌 Complications
  • Nephropathy: ACE-I/ARB first-line; annual urine albumin/Cr
  • Retinopathy: Annual dilated eye exam; anti-VEGF if proliferative
  • Neuropathy: Monofilament exam; duloxetine or gabapentin for pain
  • DKA: Anion gap acidosis + ketonemia; insulin + NS + K+ replacement
  • HHS: Glucose >600, no acidosis; aggressive fluids first
📋 Monitoring Checklist
  • HbA1c every 3 months if uncontrolled; 6 months if at goal
  • BP every visit; target <130/80
  • Lipids annually; statin if age ≥40 with DM
  • eGFR + urine albumin annually
  • Annual dilated eye exam
  • Comprehensive foot exam annually; inspect every visit
  • Vaccines: Flu, Pneumococcal, Hep B, COVID, Zoster
DKA vs HHS: DKA: glucose 250-500, pH <7.3, bicarb <18, ketones positive, anion gap >12 - treat with insulin drip + NS + K+. HHS: glucose >600, pH normal, minimal ketones, severe dehydration, AMS - aggressive IV fluids first, then insulin.
