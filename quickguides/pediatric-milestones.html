4 monthsDoes not hold head steady; does not coo/babble; does not bring objects to mouth; does not push down with legs
6 monthsDoes not roll; does not laugh/squeal; no interest in people/faces; stiff muscles or very floppy
9 monthsDoes not bear weight on legs; no babbling; does not play any back-and-forth games
12 monthsNo pointing; no waving; no words; no crawling; cannot stand with support
18 monthsFewer than 6 words; not walking; no pointing to show; does not notice when caregiver leaves
24 monthsFewer than 50 words; no 2-word phrases; does not follow 2-step instructions; loss of skills
Any ageLoss of previously acquired skills = immediate referral
Autism Screen: M-CHAT-R/F at 18 and 24 months. Red flags: no pointing by 12 months, no 2-word phrases by 24 months, loss of language skills, no response to name, lack of eye contact, repetitive behaviors.
🧠 High-Yield Pearls for Boards
  • Rule of 4s: 4 months = rolls, 4 words at 18 months - use age-specific mnemonics
  • Gross motor mnemonic: 2-4-6-9-12-15-18 (crawl at 9, walk at 12, run at 15-18 months)
  • Pincer grasp: Develops at 9-10 months (critical for self-feeding safety)
  • Language rule: Number of words approximately = age in months (up to ~24 months)
  • 2 years: Should use 2-word phrases; 75% intelligible to family
  • Object permanence: ~8-9 months (know it exists even when hidden)
  • Stranger anxiety: Peaks 8-10 months, resolves by 2-3 years
  • Separation anxiety: Peaks 12-18 months; if excessive after 3 years, evaluate
  • Parallel play: 2 years; cooperative play: 3-4 years
  • Denver II: Screening tool used at well-child visits; tests 125 milestones
