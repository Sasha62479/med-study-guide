Pediatric Developmental Milestones
AAP / CDC 2023 — Key milestones by domain and age for clinical screening

2 Months

Social / Emotional

  • Smiles at people (social smile)
  • Can be calmed by caregiver
  • Looks at face

Language

  • Coos, makes sounds
  • Turns head toward sounds
  • Different cries for different needs

Cognitive

  • Pays attention to faces
  • Follows things with eyes
  • Acts bored if activity doesn't change

Motor

  • Holds head up during tummy time
  • Makes smooth movements with limbs
  • Pushes down on legs when feet are flat

4 Months

Social / Emotional

  • Smiles spontaneously
  • Likes to play with people
  • Copies movements and facial expressions

Language

  • Babbles; uses consonants (ba, da)
  • Cries differently for different needs
  • Responds to sound with sound

Cognitive

  • Reaches for objects with one hand
  • Uses hands and eyes together
  • Watches a face closely

Motor

  • Holds head steady, unsupported
  • Pushes up on elbows (tummy time)
  • May roll from tummy to back
  • Brings hands to mouth

6 Months

Social / Emotional

  • Knows familiar faces; stranger anxiety begins
  • Likes to play with others
  • Responds to emotions in others

Language

  • Responds to own name
  • Strings vowels together (ah, eh, oh)
  • Makes sounds to show joy/displeasure

Cognitive

  • Looks at things nearby
  • Brings things to mouth
  • Shows curiosity; tries to get things out of reach

Motor

  • Rolls both ways (front to back, back to front)
  • Sits with support
  • Supports weight on legs when standing

9 Months

Social / Emotional

  • Stranger / separation anxiety
  • Has favorite toys and people
  • Understands "no"

Language

  • Babbles using mama/dada (nonspecific)
  • Copies sounds and gestures
  • Points at things

Cognitive

  • Object permanence begins
  • Looks for hidden objects
  • Plays peek-a-boo

Motor

  • Sits without support
  • Crawls (may vary in pattern)
  • Pulls to stand
  • Pincer grasp developing

12 Months

Social / Emotional

  • Shy or anxious with strangers
  • Cries when parent leaves
  • Prefers certain people
  • Repeats actions that get attention

Language

  • Mama/dada with meaning (specific)
  • Says at least 1 word besides mama/dada
  • Waves bye-bye
  • Understands simple instructions

Cognitive

  • Explores by banging, shaking, throwing
  • Finds hidden objects easily
  • Follows simple directions
  • Copies gestures

Motor

  • Stands momentarily without support
  • May take a few steps alone
  • Pincer grasp established
  • Drinks from a cup

18 Months

Social / Emotional

  • Plays alone near others (parallel play)
  • Points to show interest
  • Aware of others' emotions

Language

  • Speaks at least 10 words
  • Shakes head "no"
  • Points to ask for help
  • Identifies 1 body part

Cognitive

  • Knows what ordinary objects are for
  • Scribbles on own
  • Stacks 2–4 blocks

Motor

  • Walks independently
  • Climbs onto and off furniture
  • Feeds self with spoon
  • Helps undress self

2 Years (24 Months)

Social / Emotional

  • Copies others, especially older children
  • Excited around other children
  • Defiant behavior emerging

Language

  • Speaks at least 50 words
  • 2-word phrases (more milk, go bye-bye)
  • 50% intelligible to strangers
  • Follows 2-step instructions

Cognitive

  • Finds hidden objects with 2 steps
  • Sorts shapes and colors
  • Completes sentences in familiar books
  • Builds tower of 4+ blocks

Motor

  • Runs; kicks a ball
  • Walks up and down stairs with support
  • Turns over a container to pour out contents
  • Begins to run

No two-word phrases by 24 months: refer for speech-language evaluation. Screen for autism spectrum disorder at 18 and 24 months using M-CHAT-R/F.

3 Years

Social / Emotional

  • Copies adults and friends
  • Shows affection for friends
  • Understands "mine" and "his/hers"
  • Shows wide range of emotions

Language

  • 2–3 word sentences
  • 75% intelligible to strangers
  • Follows 2–3 step instructions
  • Knows name, age, gender

Cognitive

  • Works toys with buttons and levers
  • Plays make-believe
  • Completes 3–4 piece puzzles
  • Understands "same" vs. "different"

Motor

  • Climbs well
  • Rides tricycle
  • Walks up/down stairs alternating feet
  • Draws a circle

4 Years

Social / Emotional

  • Enjoys playing with other children
  • Cooperative play
  • Negotiates solutions to conflicts
  • Understands rules of simple games

Language

  • Tells stories
  • Sings songs/rhymes from memory
  • 100% intelligible to strangers
  • Knows first and last name

Cognitive

  • Understands counting
  • Draws a person with 2–4 body parts
  • Recognizes some letters and numbers
  • Understands time concepts (today, later)

Motor

  • Hops on one foot
  • Catches a ball most of the time
  • Uses scissors
  • Draws circle and square

5 Years

Social / Emotional

  • Wants to please friends
  • Aware of gender
  • Distinguishes fantasy from reality

Language

  • Speaks in complete sentences
  • Tells a simple story with beginning/end
  • Uses future tense
  • Prints some letters; recognizes rhymes

Cognitive

  • Counts to 10+
  • Draws a person with 6+ body parts
  • Copies triangle and other shapes
  • Knows about everyday things (money, food)

Motor

  • Stands on one foot for 10+ seconds
  • Hops, skips, somersaults
  • Uses fork, spoon, sometimes knife
  • Writes some letters; draws recognizable person

Red Flags for Developmental Delay

Language Red Flags

  • No babbling by 12 months
  • No single words by 16 months
  • No 2-word phrases by 24 months
  • Loss of previously acquired language at any age
  • Not using gestures (pointing, waving) by 12 months

Motor Red Flags

  • Not sitting by 9 months
  • Not standing by 12 months
  • Not walking by 18 months
  • Persistent toe walking after 2 years
  • Loss of previously acquired motor skills

Social / ASD Flags

  • No social smile by 6 months
  • No eye contact or pointing by 12 months
  • No imaginative play by 18 months
  • Not interested in other children
  • Repetitive behaviors, restricted interests

Cognitive / Global Delay

  • Delay across multiple domains
  • Regression at any age is always concerning
  • Failure to follow simple commands by 18 months
  • Not copying simple actions by 12 months

Regression at any age (loss of previously acquired skills) always warrants urgent evaluation. Screen all children for autism at 18 and 24 months with M-CHAT-R/F.

Developmental Screening Tools

  • M-CHAT-R/F (Modified Checklist for Autism in Toddlers): used at 18 and 24 months
  • ASQ-3 (Ages and Stages Questionnaire): broad screen at 9, 18, 30 months; parent-completed
  • Denver Developmental Screening Test II: covers 0–6 years, 4 domains
  • PEDS (Parents' Evaluation of Developmental Status): any age, parent-reported
  • Bright Futures (AAP): recommends formal developmental surveillance at every well-child visit

Primitive Reflexes

Normal Reflexes and Expected Disappearance

  • Moro reflex: disappears by 4–6 months
  • Rooting / sucking: disappears by 4–6 months
  • Palmar grasp: disappears by 5–6 months
  • Asymmetric tonic neck (fencing): disappears by 6 months
  • Plantar grasp: disappears by 9–12 months
  • Parachute reflex: appears by 9 months, persists

Clinical Significance

  • Persistence beyond expected age suggests CNS abnormality
  • Absent Moro in newborn: rule out brachial plexus injury or clavicle fracture
  • Asymmetric reflex: consider hemiparesis
  • Loss of parachute: concerning for regression

Key Clinical Pearls

  • Developmental delay: performance significantly below average in one or more domains for age
  • Premature infants: use corrected age until 24 months for milestone assessment
  • Language milestones are among the most sensitive indicators of global development
  • Social smile by 6 weeks is reassuring; absent by 3 months is concerning
  • Object permanence begins around 8–9 months (basis for peek-a-boo)
  • Stranger anxiety peaks at 9–12 months; expected and normal
  • Parallel play is normal at 18 months–3 years; cooperative play develops after 3 years
  • Hearing loss is the most common missed cause of language delay — always screen
