HFrEF: Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT)

Drug ClassExamplesMortality BenefitKey Points

Beta-blockersCarvedilol, metoprolol succinate (Toprol-XL), bisoprolol — ONLY these 3 proven in HFYes — 34% reduction in mortality (MERIT-HF, COPERNICUS, CIBIS-II)Initiate when euvolemic (NOT during acute decompensation); start low, titrate over weeks. Do NOT stop abruptly. Hold if HR <60 or systolic BP <90
MRA (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist)Spironolactone (Aldactone), eplerenone (Inspra)Yes — RALES (spironolactone): 30% mortality reduction; EPHESUS (eplerenone post-MI)Indicated when EF ≤35% + symptomatic. SGLT-2 inhibitorsDapagliflozin (Farxiga), empagliflozin (Jardiance)Yes — DAPA-HF/EMPEROR-Reduced: reduce HHF and CV death regardless of diabetes statusNew 4th pillar of GDMT; also benefit HFpEF (EMPEROR-Preserved, DELIVER trials). Start at eGFR ≥20

Ivabradine (Corlanor)IvabradineYes — reduce HHF (SHIFT trial)If (funny current) inhibitor → reduces HR without affecting contractility; indicated if: HR ≥70 bpm on max beta-blocker, SR, EF ≤35%, symptomatic HF

🎯 Boards PearlBeta-blockers contraindicated in: decompensated HF (wet, edematous — wait until euvolemic), cardiogenic shock, 2nd/3rd degree heart block (without pacemaker), active bronchospasm/severe asthma. Beta-blocker withdrawal syndrome: abrupt cessation → rebound tachycardia, hypertension, angina, MI — ALWAYS taper over 1–2 weeks when discontinuing.

🏥 Statin Side EffectsMyopathy/myalgia (1–5%): mild CK elevation; discontinue if CK >10× ULN or symptomatic. Rhabdomyolysis (rare). Risk increased with: high dose, drug interactions (fibrates, niacin, azithromycin, amiodarone, CYP3A4 inhibitors with simvastatin/lovastatin). Hepatotoxicity: rare; do NOT routinely monitor LFTs unless symptomatic. Statin-associated new-onset DM: slight increased risk (~9%); risk:benefit strongly favors statin use. Teratogenic (Category X): discontinue in pregnancy. Add ezetimibe or PCSK9 inhibitor (alirocumab, evolocumab) if LDL goal not achieved on maximally-tolerated statin.

ACS Acute Pharmacotherapy

🏥 STEMI/NSTEMI Acute Drug Protocol: MONA + DAPT + Heparin + High-Intensity StatinMorphine (severe pain; may worsen NSTEMI outcomes — use selectively) · Oxygen (if SaO2 <90%) · Nitroglycerin (SL/IV — angina relief, preload reduction; avoid if hypotension, inferior MI with RV involvement, recent PDE5 inhibitor use within 24–48h) · Aspirin 325mg (loading dose immediately) · THEN: P2Y12 inhibitor (ticagrelor 180mg or prasugrel 60mg loading dose for STEMI/PCI; clopidogrel 300–600mg if others contraindicated) · Anticoagulant (UFH IV bolus 60 units/kg for STEMI/PCI, or enoxaparin for NSTEMI) · High-intensity statin (atorvastatin 80mg or rosuvastatin 40mg) · Beta-blocker (within 24h if no contraindications) · ACE-I (within 24h for anterior MI, EF <40%, HF, or diabetes)
