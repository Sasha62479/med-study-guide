Combined Oral Contraceptives (COCs)

Mechanism

Estrogen + progestin → suppress LH surge → prevent ovulation; thicken cervical mucus; thin endometrium

ComponentEffectSide Effects
Estrogen (ethinyl estradiol)Suppress FSH/LH; stabilize endometriumNausea, breast tenderness, thromboembolic risk
ProgestinSuppress LH; cervical mucus thickeningMood changes, acne, decreased libido

Contraindications (ACHES Mnemonic)

MnemonicA – Abdominal pain (thrombosis); C – Chest pain (MI/PE); H – Headache severe (stroke); E – Eye changes (vision loss); S – Severe leg pain (DVT)
Boards PearlCOCs are CONTRAINDICATED in: smokers ≥35 yo, migraines with aura, personal hx DVT/PE, active liver disease, uncontrolled HTN, ischemic heart disease, breast cancer. Also avoid within 3 weeks postpartum (if not breastfeeding) due to VTE risk.

Non-Contraceptive Benefits

  • Reduce ovarian and endometrial cancer risk
  • Treat dysmenorrhea, endometriosis, PCOS (acne, irregular cycles)
  • Decrease menstrual blood loss (anemia)
  • Suppress functional ovarian cysts

Progestin-Only Pills (POPs / "Mini-Pill")

Norethindrone — primarily thickens cervical mucus; must be taken same time daily (±3 hr window); safe in breastfeeding, smokers ≥35, migraine with aura, HTN

Boards PearlNorethindrone POP: no estrogen CI applies. If pill taken >3 hrs late → use backup for 48 hrs. Irregular bleeding is common side effect.

Spironolactone for PCOS/Hirsutism

Aldosterone antagonist + anti-androgen → blocks androgen receptors → reduces hirsutism, acne in PCOS. Dose: 50–200 mg/day. Monitor K⁺ (hyperkalemia risk). CI in pregnancy (feminizes male fetus). Combine with OCP for contraception.

Boards PearlSpironolactone = first-line for hirsutism in PCOS when OCPs insufficient. Also used for female-pattern hair loss. Monitor potassium, especially if on ACE-I/ARB.

GnRH Agonists & Antagonists

Leuprolide (Lupron) — GnRH Agonist

Continuous administration → downregulates GnRH receptors → ↓ FSH/LH → ↓ estrogen/testosterone ("medical castration")

Indications
  • Endometriosis (reduces lesion size, pain)
  • Uterine fibroids (preoperative shrinkage)
  • Precocious puberty
  • Prostate cancer (androgen deprivation)
  • Breast cancer (premenopausal, hormone receptor+)
  • Fertility treatment (prevent premature LH surge)
Boards PearlInitial "flare effect" — first 1–2 weeks of leuprolide causes transient ↑ testosterone (can worsen prostate cancer symptoms). Prevent with anti-androgen (bicalutamide) for first 2–4 weeks. Long-term: hot flashes, bone loss, decreased libido.

Elagolix (Orilissa) — GnRH Antagonist

Immediate suppression (no flare); oral; indicated for endometriosis pain. Lower dose: partial estrogen suppression. Higher dose: full suppression (used ≤6 months due to bone loss).

Methotrexate for Ectopic Pregnancy

Folate antagonist → inhibits DNA synthesis → kills rapidly dividing trophoblastic cells

Criteria for Medical MgmtHemodynamically stable; unruptured; hCG <5000 (ideally <3000); no cardiac activity on US; no significant free fluid; willing/able to follow-up
Boards PearlAfter MTX for ectopic: avoid NSAIDs (reduce efficacy), folate supplements, alcohol, sunlight. Serial hCG q48h — should decrease ≥15% from day 4→7. If not, repeat dose or surgery. Contraindicated: immunodeficiency, thrombocytopenia, hepatic/renal disease, breastfeeding, intrauterine pregnancy.

Recurrent UTI Prophylaxis

  • Nitrofurantoin 50–100mg QHS (continuous) or post-coital
  • TMP-SMX 40/200mg QHS
  • Vaginal estrogen in postmenopausal women (reduces recurrence significantly)
  • D-mannose (OTC; some evidence for prevention)

OB Medications

Oxytocin (Pitocin)

Synthetic oxytocin → uterine smooth muscle contraction via oxytocin receptors; also used for postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) prevention

IndicationUseKey Points
Labor induction/augmentationIV infusion, titratedRisk: uterine hyperstimulation, fetal distress, water retention (antidiuretic effect at high doses)
Postpartum hemorrhage10–40 units IV or IMFirst-line PPH treatment; uterine atony = #1 cause PPH
Incomplete abortionIV infusionPromotes uterine contraction to expel retained products

Magnesium Sulfate (MgSO₄)

Indications
  • Eclampsia prevention and treatment (seizes prophylaxis in severe preeclampsia)
  • Neuroprotection for premature infant (<32 weeks gestation)
  • Tocolysis (short-term, 48 hours — not first-line)
Boards PearlMg toxicity progression: Loss of DTRs (9–12 mEq/L) → respiratory depression (12–15 mEq/L) → cardiac arrest (>15 mEq/L). Antidote: Calcium gluconate 1g IV. Monitor UO, DTRs, RR. Therapeutic level for seizure prophylaxis: 4–7 mEq/L.

Antenatal Corticosteroids

Betamethasone 12mg IM q24h x2 doses (or dexamethasone 6mg IM q12h x4 doses) — for fetal lung maturation when preterm birth anticipated at 24–34 weeks. Reduces RDS, IVH, necrotizing enterocolitis, and neonatal mortality.

Combination Therapy & PDE5 Inhibitors

Alpha-blocker + 5-ARI (e.g., Jalyn = dutasteride + tamsulosin): most effective for large prostates, reduces risk of acute urinary retention and need for surgery (CombAT trial).
Tadalafil (Cialis) 5mg daily: PDE5 inhibitor approved for BPH ± erectile dysfunction; relaxes prostate/bladder smooth muscle; do NOT combine with nitrates (risk of severe hypotension).
