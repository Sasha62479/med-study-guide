Lung Zones (West)

Pulmonary blood flow distribution is gravity-dependent and described by West zones:

ZoneLocationPA vs Pa vs PvFlow
Zone 1ApexPA > Pa > PvNo flow (physiologic dead space); increased in +PPV, hypovolemia
Zone 2MiddlePa > PA > PvIntermittent (Starling resistor)
Zone 3BasePa > Pv > PAContinuous; best V/Q match; best perfusion

PA = alveolar pressure; Pa = arterial pressure; Pv = venous pressure. Zone 3 is the largest in an upright individual at rest.

⭐ Boards Pearl

V/Q mismatch: Zone 1 = high V/Q (dead space). Zone 3 = low V/Q (shunt-like). Blood gas shows hypoxemia that corrects with O2 in dead space (V/Q mismatch) but NOT in true shunt.

🫁

Gross Anatomy

The right lung (3 lobes: upper, middle, lower; 10 bronchopulmonary segments) is larger than the left lung (2 lobes: upper, lower; 8-9 segments; cardiac notch + lingula).

Hilum Contents

  • Right hilum: Pulmonary artery (anterior) → eparterial bronchus → hyparterial bronchus + pulmonary vein (inferior)
  • Left hilum: Pulmonary artery (most superior) → bronchus → pulmonary veins
  • Mnemonic: RALS = Right Artery Left Superior (pulmonary artery on right is anterior; on left is superior)

Pleura

  • Visceral pleura — covers lung surface; no pain fibers
  • Parietal pleura — lines thoracic wall, diaphragm, mediastinum; has pain fibers (somatic)
  • Pleural space — potential space; 10-20 mL fluid normally; negative pressure (-5 cmH2O)
🩺 Clinical

Tension pneumothorax: tracheal deviation AWAY from affected side, decreased breath sounds, hypoxia, JVD, hypotension. Needle decompression at 2nd ICS MCL before chest tube.

⭐ Boards Pearl

The carina angle (right main bronchus is ~25° from vertical, left ~45°) means foreign bodies preferentially fall into the right main bronchus and right lower lobe. Aspirated food → right lung, right lower lobe (upright) or right upper lobe (supine).

Microscopic Anatomy

Pneumocytes

Cell% Alveolar SurfaceFunction
Type I~95%Gas exchange; thin squamous; cannot replicate
Type II~5%Surfactant production; repair/replicate after injury; cuboidal (lamellar bodies)

Surfactant

  • Dipalmitoylphosphatidylcholine (DPPC) — main component; reduces surface tension
  • Produced by Type II pneumocytes; secreted at ~28+ weeks gestation
  • Lowers surface tension → prevents alveolar collapse (atelectasis) on expiration
⭐ Boards Pearl

Neonatal RDS: premature infants lack surfactant → ground-glass opacities on CXR; grunting, nasal flaring, intercostal retractions. Treatment: maternal betamethasone (accelerates surfactant production), exogenous surfactant. Surfactant: 28 wks, lecithin/sphingomyelin ratio >2 = lung maturity.

Other Key Cells

  • Alveolar macrophages — phagocytose debris/bacteria; become "heart failure cells" (hemosiderin-laden) in pulmonary edema
  • Clara (Club) cells — non-ciliated; in terminal/respiratory bronchioles; detoxify inhaled toxins; secrete CC16 protein
🩸

Pulmonary Vasculature

The pulmonary circulation is a LOW-pressure, HIGH-flow system (PA systolic ~25 mmHg).

  • Pulmonary arteries — carry deoxygenated blood from RV to alveolar capillaries
  • Pulmonary veins — carry oxygenated blood from lungs to LA (4 veins)
  • Bronchial arteries — from thoracic aorta; supply lung parenchyma, pleura, large airways

Hypoxic Pulmonary Vasoconstriction

Local alveolar hypoxia → vasoconstriction of pulmonary arterioles → diverts blood to better-ventilated areas → improves V/Q matching. Opposite of systemic circulation response to hypoxia.

🩺 Clinical

Pulmonary hypertension (PH): mean PA >20 mmHg. Group 1 (PAH) = idiopathic, connective tissue, HIV, portopulmonary. Groups 2-5 include left heart disease, lung disease, chronic thromboembolism, unclear mechanisms. Treatment: PDE5 inhibitors, endothelin receptor antagonists, prostacyclins.

Embryology

Lungs develop from a ventral outgrowth of the foregut endoderm (laryngotracheal groove) at ~week 4-5. Surrounding splanchnic mesoderm forms cartilage, smooth muscle, and blood vessels.

Stages of Lung Development

  • Embryonic (0-7 wks) — tracheal bud, lobar/segmental bronchi
  • Pseudoglandular (7-17 wks) — conducting airways form; no gas exchange
  • Canalicular (16-25 wks) — acini form; vascularization; begins limited viability
  • Terminal sac/Saccular (24-36 wks) — primitive alveoli; surfactant begins ~28 wks
  • Alveolar (36 wks - 8 years) — mature alveoli form; most postnatal alveologenesis
⭐ Boards Pearl

Tracheoesophageal fistula (TEF): most common type = esophageal atresia + distal TEF (Type C, 85%). Presents with 3 Cs: Coughing, Choking, Cyanosis with feeding. Polyhydramnios in utero (fetus can't swallow amniotic fluid).
