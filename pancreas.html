Gross Anatomy

Retroperitoneal mixed gland (~15 cm long) from duodenal C-loop to splenic hilum.

Parts

  • Head — in C-loop of duodenum; CBD passes through; uncinate process
  • Neck — over SMA/SMV junction
  • Body — crosses vertebral column
  • Tail — touches splenic hilum; most mobile

Duct System

  • Main duct (Wirsung) — joins CBD at ampulla of Vater; enters D2
  • Accessory duct (Santorini) — drains head; opens at minor papilla
RegionArterial Supply
HeadSuperior/inferior pancreaticoduodenal aa.
Body/TailSplenic artery branches
🩺 Clinical

Courvoisier sign: painless palpable GB + jaundice = pancreatic head cancer until proven otherwise.

⚗️

Exocrine Function

~98% of gland. Acinar cells produce enzymes; ductal cells secrete HCO3-rich fluid.

Acinar Cell Products (as zymogens)

  • Trypsinogen, chymotrypsinogen, proelastase, procarboxypeptidase
  • Pancreatic lipase, phospholipase A2 (active form)
  • Amylase (active form; digests starch)

Activation: Enterokinase (duodenal brush border) activates trypsinogen → trypsin activates all others

⭐ Boards Pearl

GET SMASHED: Gallstones, Ethanol, Trauma, Steroids, Mumps, Autoimmune, Scorpion, Hyperlipidemia/Hypercalcemia, ERCP, Drugs — causes of pancreatitis

Secretin (duodenal S-cells) → ductal HCO3- secretion. CCK (I-cells) → enzyme secretion.

🔬

Endocrine Function

Islets of Langerhans (~1-2% of gland; concentrated in tail). Hormones drain into portal circulation.

⭐ Boards Pearl

C-peptide distinguishes endogenous (HIGH) vs exogenous insulin (LOW). Insulinoma = high insulin + high C-peptide. Factitious hypoglycemia = high insulin + LOW C-peptide.

🧠 Mnemonic

Islet layout outside-in: "DAB" — Delta, Alpha, Beta (center). Beta cells are in the center like a Bear in a cave.

🧬

Embryology

Two endodermal buds from the foregut fuse at week 7:

  • Dorsal bud → body, tail, superior head (Santorini duct)
  • Ventral bud (rotates with bile duct) → uncinate + inferior head (Wirsung duct)
⭐ Boards Pearl

Annular pancreas: ventral bud fails to rotate → ring around duodenum → double-bubble sign in neonates. Pancreas divisum: ducts fail to fuse → most common congenital pancreatic anomaly → chronic pancreatitis risk.
