Gross Anatomy

The heart is a four-chambered muscular organ (~300 g) located in the middle mediastinum. It lies obliquely: apex points left/inferiorly (5th ICS, MCL); base faces right/superiorly.

Chambers

ChamberWall ThicknessKey Feature
Right atriumThinSVC/IVC/coronary sinus inflow; fossa ovalis (closed foramen ovale)
Right ventricle~3-4 mmCrista supraventricularis; trabecular (spongy); moderator band
Left atriumThin4 pulmonary veins; posterior-most chamber
Left ventricle~8-12 mmThickest; smooth outflow tract; papillary muscles

Pericardium

  • Fibrous pericardium — tough outer layer; attaches to diaphragm
  • Parietal pericardium — inner lining of fibrous layer
  • Visceral pericardium (epicardium) — directly on heart surface; contains coronary vessels and fat
  • Pericardial cavity — 15-50 mL serous fluid; reduces friction
🩺 Clinical

Cardiac tamponade: Beck's triad = hypotension + JVD + muffled heart sounds. Pulsus paradoxus (>10 mmHg drop in SBP during inspiration). Pericardiocentesis at left 5th ICS or subxiphoid approach.

⭐ Boards Pearl

S1 = mitral + tricuspid closure (AV valves close at start of systole). S2 = aortic + pulmonic closure (semilunar valves close at start of diastole). S3 (ventricular filling) = HF/volume overload. S4 (atrial kick against stiff ventricle) = HTN, AS, HCM.

🧠 Mnemonic

Valve locations (auscultation): "APT M" clockwise: Aortic (R 2nd ICS), Pulmonic (L 2nd ICS), Tricuspid (L lower sternal border), Mitral (L 5th ICS MCL = apex).

Coronary Arteries

ArteryBranchesTerritory

LCA → LCxObtuse marginalsLateral/posterior LV wall
RCASA nodal (60%), AV nodal (85%), marginals, PDARV, inferior LV, posterior IVS (PDA), SA/AV nodes

Dominance: Determined by which artery gives off the PDA (posterior descending artery). Right dominant (~70%), left dominant (~10%), co-dominant (~20%).

⭐ Boards Pearl

MI territory: LAD = anterior MI (leads II, III, aVF ≠ anterior — that's V1-V4). RCA = inferior MI (II, III, aVF); also right-sided leads if RV involved. LCx = lateral MI (I, aVL, V5-V6). RCA occlusion → AV block (nodal ischemia).

🩺 Clinical

Wolff-Parkinson-White: accessory pathway (Bundle of Kent) bypasses AV node → delta wave on EKG, short PR interval, wide QRS. Risk of SVT and sudden death. Treatment: ablation.

Microscopic Anatomy

Cardiac Muscle

  • Branching cells (not parallel like skeletal muscle)
  • Single central nucleus
  • Intercalated discs — contain gap junctions (electrical coupling) and desmosomes (mechanical coupling)
  • T-tubules at Z lines (vs. A-I junction in skeletal muscle)
  • Abundant mitochondria (~30% cell volume) — high aerobic demands
⭐ Boards Pearl

Cardiac muscle uses gap junctions for electrical syncytium — action potential spreads throughout atria as a unit, then through AV node delay, then through ventricles. Gap junction protein = connexin 43.

Frank-Starling Relationship

Increased preload (end-diastolic volume/stretch) → increased stroke volume. Operates through length-dependent activation of myosin-actin crossbridges.

🧬

Embryology

Heart develops from splanchnic lateral plate mesoderm (cardiogenic region) by ~week 3; first organ to function (~day 22).

  • Two heart tubes fuse → single primitive heart tube
  • Cardiac looping (week 4): tube bends rightward (D-loop) → forms cardiac chambers
  • Septa formation: septum primum + secundum → atrial septum; muscular + membranous IVS → ventricular septum
  • Foramen ovale — R→L shunt in fetus (bypasses pulmonary circulation); closes at birth due to ↑LA pressure
  • Ductus arteriosus — shunts blood from PA to aorta in fetus; closes with ↑O2 and ↓PGE2 at birth; indomethacin closes PDA; PGE1 keeps it open
⭐ Boards Pearl

L-TGA (dextro-looping) = aorta anterior-right, PA posterior-left = most common cause of cyanosis in the first 24 hours. VSD = most common congenital heart defect overall. ToF = most common CYANOTIC CHD after infancy.

🩺 Clinical

Cardiac biomarkers: Troponin I/T (rise 3-6h, peak 24h, normalize 7-10d) = most specific. CK-MB (rise 3-6h, normalize 48-72h) = useful for re-infarction. BNP/NT-proBNP = heart failure diagnosis and prognosis.
