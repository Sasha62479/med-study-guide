Gross Anatomy

Location & Size

The liver is the largest internal organ (~1500 g) located in the right upper quadrant under the diaphragm. It has four lobes: right (largest), left, caudate, and quadrate.

Lobar Anatomy

  • Falciform ligament — separates right and left lobes on the anterior surface; contains the ligamentum teres (remnant of umbilical vein)
  • Porta hepatis — hilum containing portal vein, hepatic artery, and bile duct (portal triad)
  • Bare area — peritoneum-free area on posterior surface in direct contact with the diaphragm

Blood Supply

Vessel% O₂ Supply% Blood FlowNotes
Portal vein50–60%~75%Nutrient-rich blood from GI tract & spleen
Hepatic artery40–50%~25%Branch of celiac trunk; O₂-rich
🩺 Clinical

The liver has a dual blood supply — portal hypertension results when portal venous pressure rises, causing varices, splenomegaly, and ascites.

Microscopic Anatomy

Hepatic Lobule

The classic lobule is hexagonal with a central vein and portal triads at each corner. Hepatocytes are arranged in cords (plates) that radiate from the central vein.

Acinar Zones (Rappaport)

ZoneLocationO₂ LevelVulnerable To
Zone 1Near portal triadHighestToxic injury (acetaminophen? No — Zone 3)
Zone 2IntermediateIntermediateYellow fever necrosis
Zone 3Near central veinLowestIschemic necrosis; acetaminophen toxicity; alcohol
⭐ Boards Pearl

Zone 3 (centrilobular) = site of acetaminophen toxicity, alcoholic liver disease, and ischemic hepatitis. Zone 1 = toxic damage from phosphorus, beryllium, salicylates.

Cell Types

  • Hepatocytes — polygonal cells; main metabolic/synthetic functions; 80% of liver mass
  • Kupffer cells — liver-resident macrophages in sinusoids; phagocytose bacteria, debris
  • Stellate (Ito) cells — store vitamin A (retinol); activated in fibrosis → produce collagen
  • Sinusoidal endothelial cells — fenestrated; no basement membrane; allow plasma-hepatocyte exchange
  • Space of Disse — perisinusoidal space between hepatocytes and sinusoidal endothelium
🧠 Mnemonic

"Ito = Italian sculptor" (Ito cells sculpt/scar the liver) — stellate cells store fat and vitamin A, but when activated produce collagen causing fibrosis/cirrhosis.

⚙️

Function

Key Functions

  • Carbohydrate metabolism — glycogenesis, glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis
  • Protein synthesis — albumin, clotting factors (I, II, V, VII, IX, X, XI), complement proteins, transport proteins
  • Lipid metabolism — fatty acid oxidation, ketogenesis, lipoprotein synthesis (VLDL), cholesterol synthesis
  • Detoxification — cytochrome P450 system; first-pass metabolism; ammonia → urea (urea cycle)
  • Bile production — ~600–1000 mL/day; bile salts emulsify dietary fats
  • Storage — glycogen, triglycerides, vitamins A, D, E, K, B12, iron (ferritin)
  • Bilirubin metabolism — unconjugated → conjugated (water-soluble) → secreted into bile

Ethanol Metabolism

Ethanol is metabolized primarily in the liver via two pathways:

  • Alcohol dehydrogenase (ADH) — Ethanol → Acetaldehyde → Acetate (by ALDH)
  • Microsomal ethanol-oxidizing system (MEOS) — CYP2E1; produces free radicals; inducible by chronic use
⭐ Boards Pearl

Alcohol metabolism increases NADH/NAD⁺ ratio → inhibits gluconeogenesis (hypoglycemia) and fatty acid oxidation → fatty liver (steatosis). Acetaldehyde is directly hepatotoxic.

Progression of Alcoholic Liver Disease

Steatosis → Alcoholic hepatitis (AST:ALT >2:1) → Cirrhosis → Hepatocellular carcinoma

🩺 Clinical

AST:ALT ratio >2:1 is a classic boards pearl for alcoholic hepatitis. Elevated GGT is also sensitive for alcohol use. Total bilirubin rises with worsening hepatocellular dysfunction.

🧬

Embryology

The liver develops from the hepatic diverticulum (liver bud), an outgrowth of the foregut endoderm at ~4 weeks gestation.

  • Hepatic bud → liver parenchyma (hepatocytes) and intrahepatic bile ducts
  • Mesoderm of septum transversum → Kupffer cells, stellate cells, and connective tissue
  • Ligamentum teres — remnant of umbilical vein (carries oxygenated blood to fetus)
  • Ligamentum venosum — remnant of ductus venosus (fetal bypass of hepatic sinusoids)
🧠 Mnemonic

"Liver = Foregut" (hepatic diverticulum grows from foregut) — remember: liver and pancreas both bud off the foregut at ~week 4.

⭐ Boards Pearl

Budd-Chiari syndrome = hepatic vein thrombosis → tender hepatomegaly, ascites, and elevated liver enzymes without portal hypertension signs early on. Associated with hypercoagulable states.

🩺 Clinical

Child-Pugh score assesses severity of cirrhosis using: Bilirubin, Albumin, PT/INR, Ascites, Encephalopathy. MELD score predicts 90-day mortality and is used for transplant prioritization.
