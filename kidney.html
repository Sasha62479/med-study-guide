Gross Anatomy

Paired retroperitoneal organs (~10-12 cm long, 150 g each). Right kidney is slightly lower due to liver. T12-L3 vertebral levels.

External Anatomy

  • Renal capsule — fibrous outer covering
  • Perirenal fat — cushions the kidney within Gerota fascia
  • Hilum — medial indentation; contains renal vein (anterior), artery (middle), ureter (posterior) — VAU

Internal Anatomy

  • Cortex — outer; contains glomeruli, PCT, DCT, cortical collecting ducts
  • Medulla — inner; contains loops of Henle, vasa recta, medullary collecting ducts
  • Renal pyramids — medullary triangles; apex = renal papilla → drains into minor calyx → major calyx → renal pelvis → ureter
  • Columns of Bertin — cortical tissue between pyramids

⭐ Boards Pearl

Renal arteries are end arteries → renal infarcts are well-defined wedge-shaped. No anastomoses = ligation of any segmental artery = irreversible ischemia to that segment.

🔬

Nephron

~1 million nephrons per kidney. Each nephron = glomerulus + tubule. Two types:

  • Cortical nephrons (~85%) — short loops of Henle; juxtamedullary cortex
  • Juxtamedullary nephrons (~15%) — long loops deep into medulla; key for urinary concentration

🧠 Mnemonic

Loop diuretics: furosemide blocks NKCC2 in thick ascending limb. "LASIX = Loop, Ascending, SIX things it blocks." Thiazides block NCC in DCT. Amiloride/triamterene block ENaC in collecting duct.

🧫

Glomerular Filtration Barrier

Three layers selectively filter ~180 L/day of plasma based on size and charge:

  • Fenestrated endothelium — 70-100 nm pores; prevents cells from passing; surface coated with negatively charged glycocalyx
  • GBM (glomerular basement membrane) — type IV collagen + laminin + heparan sulfate (negative charge → repels albumin)
  • Podocytes (visceral epithelium) — foot processes with filtration slits (25-60 nm); nephrin bridges slit diaphragm; most important size barrier
Passes FreelyPartially RestrictedExcluded
Water, electrolytes, glucose, urea, creatinine, inulinMyoglobin (small)Albumin, globulins, cells
🩺 Clinical

Loss of GBM negative charge (e.g., minimal change disease) → albumin leaks → nephrotic syndrome (proteinuria >3.5 g/day, hypoalbuminemia, edema, hyperlipidemia). Nephrin mutations → congenital nephrotic syndrome.

GFR Estimation

GFR ≈ 125 mL/min normal. Estimated by CKD-EPI or MDRD using serum creatinine, age, sex, race. Cystatin C more accurate in some populations. Inulin clearance = gold standard.

⚡

Juxtaglomerular Complex

Specialized structure at the vascular pole of the glomerulus regulating GFR and BP.

  • JG cells (granular cells) — modified smooth muscle in afferent arteriole wall; secrete renin in response to: ↓BP, ↑sympathetics, ↓NaCl delivery to macula densa
  • Macula densa — specialized DCT cells sensing NaCl concentration; ↓NaCl → signals JG cells to release renin
  • Lacis (extraglomerular mesangial) cells — between macula densa and JG cells; signaling role

RAAS Cascade

Renin → Angiotensinogen → Angiotensin I → (ACE in lung) → Angiotensin II → ↑Aldosterone (adrenal) + ↑vasoconstriction + ↑ADH + ↑thirst

⭐ Boards Pearl

ACE inhibitors: block Ang II formation → ↓efferent arteriole tone → ↓GFR → creatinine may rise initially. Safe in most HTN/CHF/CKD but contraindicated in bilateral renal artery stenosis (precipitates acute renal failure) and pregnancy.

🧬

Embryology

Kidney develops from intermediate mesoderm through three successive stages:

  • Pronephros — week 4; rudimentary; regresses completely; no clinical significance
  • Mesonephros — weeks 4-8; temporary function; contributes to male genital ducts (epididymis, vas deferens)
  • Metanephros — permanent kidney; two components:
    • Ureteric bud (from mesonephric duct) → ureter, renal pelvis, calyces, collecting ducts
    • Metanephric mesenchyme → glomerulus, PCT, loop of Henle, DCT
⭐ Boards Pearl

Horseshoe kidney: fusion of lower poles during ascent; caught on IMA → stays at L3. Most common renal fusion anomaly. Associated with Turner syndrome. Renal agenesis (Potter sequence): oligohydramnios → lung hypoplasia, limb deformities, facial anomalies.
