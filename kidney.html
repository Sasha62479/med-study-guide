Anatomy & Physiology

Kidneys

Paired retroperitoneal organs (T12-L3) responsible for waste excretion, fluid/electrolyte balance, acid-base regulation, and hormone synthesis. The nephron is the functional unit.

1. Gross Anatomy

Overview

  • Reddish-brown, bean-shaped, paired retroperitoneal organs at T12-L3
  • Each kidney: ~5-6.3 oz; Dimensions: 10-12 cm x 4-6 cm x 3-4 cm
  • Superior pole: adrenal gland
  • Left kidney anteriorly: spleen, stomach, pancreas, splenic flexure, jejunum
  • Right kidney anteriorly: right lobe of liver, descending duodenum, hepatic flexure

Internal Macrostructure

  • Renal cortex (~10 mm): outermost layer; contains glomeruli, PCT, DCT, cortical collecting ducts; extends inward as renal columns separating medullary pyramids
  • Renal medulla: consists of medullary pyramids; contains loops of Henle and collecting ducts; base faces cortex, apex (renal papilla) drains into minor calyx
  • Renal sinus: contains renal calyces and renal pelvis; minor calyces merge into major calyces into renal pelvis (proximal ureter)
  • Renal hilum: medial fissure where renal pelvis, vessels, and nerves enter/exit
Note: The kidneys receive 20-25% of cardiac output, but the medulla is relatively hypoxic and vulnerable to ischemic damage.

Surrounding Structures (outside to inside)

  • Pararenal fat: superficial to renal fascia; protects kidneys from trauma
  • Perirenal fascia (Gerota's fascia): envelops kidney and adrenal gland with perirenal fat
  • Perirenal fat: envelopes the kidney; covered by renal fascia
  • Renal capsule: thin fibrous capsule around the kidney

2. Microscopic Anatomy - The Nephron

The nephron is the functional unit of the kidney (~1 million per kidney). It consists of a renal corpuscle (filters blood) and renal tubule (forms urine).

Types of Nephrons

  • Cortical nephrons (~85%): glomerulus near outer cortex; short loop of Henle; mainly regulatory and excretory functions
  • Juxtamedullary nephrons (~15%): glomerulus near cortex-medulla junction; long loop of Henle; main function is concentration/dilution of urine

Renal Corpuscle

  • Located in renal cortex; consists of glomerulus enclosed by Bowman capsule
  • Glomerulus: tuft of capillaries; afferent arteriole enters, efferent arteriole exits; intraglomerular mesangial cells between capillaries (contractile, phagocytic)
  • Bowman capsule: outer parietal surface (simple squamous); inner visceral surface (podocytes); Bowman space between them is continuous with PCT

3. Juxtaglomerular Complex

A specialized structure between the DCT and afferent arteriole that maintains GFR by regulating the RAAS.

Components

  • Juxtaglomerular (JG) cells: modified smooth muscle cells in the afferent arterioles; synthesize and secrete renin
  • Macula densa: tall cuboidal cells at the distal end of the thick ascending loop of Henle; monitors NaCl concentration in the DCT lumen:
    • Hypoosmolar urine: triggers renin release (vasoconstriction of efferent arteriole, increases GFR)
    • Hyperosmolar urine: triggers adenosine release (vasoconstriction of afferent arteriole, decreases GFR)
  • Extraglomerular mesangial cells: connect macula densa with JG effector cells; role in autoregulation of renal blood flow

4. Embryology

Origin: intermediate mesoderm. Development begins at week 4. Precursor cells migrate cranial to caudal via the urogenital ridge.

Three Sequential Stages

StageTimingFate
PronephrosWeeks 3-4; cervical regionRudimentary; degenerates with mesonephros development
MesonephrosWeek 4; functions until end of 2nd monthCranial end: interim kidney. Caudal end: contributes to male genital system (epididymis, ductus deferens, seminal vesicles, ejaculatory duct via mesonephric duct)
MetanephrosWeek 5; permanent kidney; canalization complete by week 10; matures until weeks 35-36Forms from ureteric bud + metanephric blastema

Metanephros Components

  • Ureteric bud (metanephric diverticulum): from caudal mesonephric duct; differentiates into ureter, renal pelvis, major and minor calyces, collecting ducts
  • Metanephric blastema: stimulated by ureteric bud; differentiates into glomerulus, PCT, loop of Henle, DCT
Ureteropelvic junction (UPJ): Canalizes last; most common site of prenatal urinary tract obstruction. May cause prenatal hydronephrosis seen on ultrasound.
Horseshoe kidney: If inferior poles of both kidneys fuse during fetal development, they become trapped under the inferior mesenteric artery.

5. Clinical Significance

Congenital Anomalies

  • Autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease (ADPKD)
  • Autosomal recessive polycystic kidney disease (ARPKD)
  • Simple and complex renal cysts, horseshoe kidney

Glomerular Disease

  • Nephrotic syndrome (proteinuria, edema, hypoalbuminemia, hyperlipidemia)
  • Nephritic syndrome (hematuria, hypertension, oliguria, mild proteinuria)
  • Glomerulonephritis (IgA nephropathy, post-streptococcal, membranous, FSGS, minimal change)

Tubular and Interstitial Disease

  • Acute kidney injury (AKI), Chronic kidney disease (CKD)
  • Tubulointerstitial nephritis, Renal tubular acidosis (RTA)

Obstructive and Infectious

  • Nephrolithiasis (kidney stones), Hydronephrosis
  • Pyelonephritis (ascending UTI), Renal abscess

Tumors

  • Renal cell carcinoma (clear cell most common)
  • Nephroblastoma (Wilms tumor) - pediatric
  • Renal oncocytoma, Transitional cell carcinoma (renal pelvis)
